Construalc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/Construal

A situation constituted by the premises as perceived by the agent responsible for an Opinion. It is perspective-dependent — it reifies the state of affairs from the standpoint of the cognizer, not an objective state of affairs. A Construal can be composed of parts, each of which is itself a dul:Situation representing an individual premise (i.e., another state of affairs). The dul:Situation, part of the Construal, can be satisfied by a secondary dul:Description.

Time-Indexed Construalc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/TimeIndexedConstrual

A Construal where the perceived state of affairs holds only during a specific time interval. Inherits from both opino:Construal — carrying the perspectival and epistemic properties of a perception — and dul:TimeIndexedRelation, which provides the temporal bounding mechanism. Used when the perceived premises are not timeless observations but temporally bounded states of affairs, such as attributions, classifications, or roles that held only during a specific period.

enablesop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/enables

Relates a Construal to another Construal that it makes possible as a subsequent step in an inferential chain. A Construal enables another when it provides the evidential or logical basis from which the subsequent Construal can be constituted. For example, the Construal 'Fief is a Medieval Latin term' enables the Construal 'The Donation contains anachronistic Latin', which in turn grounds the Opinion 'The Donation is a Forgery'. Inverse counterpart of opino:presupposes in the inferential chain, though not declared as a formal OWL inverse. Subproperty of dul:hasPostcondition.

has partop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/hasPart

Relates a Construal to its constituent sub-situations, each representing an individual premise as perceived by the cognizer. The relation is recursive — a sub-Situation may itself have parts that ground it further, mirroring the CRMinf premise chain (J1 usedAsPremise). Each sub-Situation may optionally satisfy a dul:Description (e.g. a Framester frame), representing the inference logic (CRMinf J3 applies) through which that premise is interpreted. The conclusion drawn from the full Construal is always an opino:Opinion (dul:Description), connected via opino:grounds.

involvesop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/involves

Relates a Construal to any entity — object, agent, event, or abstract concept — that the cognizer perceives as participating in the construed state of affairs. Following the social psychology notion of construal (Ross 1987), the entities involved are not neutral facts but are perceived and interpreted through the cognizer's subjective lens. The Construal grounds the Opinion (subclass of Description).

presupposesop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://w3id.org/opino/presupposes

Relates a Construal to another Construal that must hold as a logical premise before it. Represents the inferential ordering between premise Construals in a chain of reasoning — the 'presupposed' Construal provides the evidential or logical ground that the dependent Construal builds upon. For example, the Construal 'Fief is a Medieval Latin term' is the presupposition of the Construal 'The Donation contains anachronistic Latin'. Subproperty of dul:hasPrecondition, defined in DUL as direct precedence applied to situations.
